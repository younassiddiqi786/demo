    A DevOps engineer is an IT generalist who should have a wide-ranging knowledge of both development and operations, including coding, infrastructure management, system administration, and DevOps toolchains.

    About DevOps Engieering

    DevOps, a portmanteau of Development and Operations, is a collaborative approach to software development and IT operations that emphasizes communication, collaboration, and automation. DevOps aims to streamline the software delivery lifecycle by breaking down silos between development and operations teams, fostering a culture of shared responsibility and continuous improvement. It promotes the use of tools and practices that automate manual processes, ensuring faster and more reliable software releases. Key DevOps principles include continuous integration, continuous delivery, and continuous monitoring, enabling teams to deliver high-quality software at a rapid pace. By integrating development and operations seamlessly, DevOps enhances agility, accelerates time-to-market, and enhances overall system reliability, fostering innovation and efficiency in the IT industry.

    DevOps Roles & Mission

    At the crossroads between software development (Dev) and IT operations (Ops), this hybrid profile has become a key position within companies looking to ensure the continuous deployment of new features. This role is essential for project teams to be more agile in a competitive and changing market.
